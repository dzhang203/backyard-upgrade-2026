# 811 "Call Before You Dig" — California Requirements

**Primary Source:** [https://call811.com/](https://call811.com/)
**California-specific operator:** [USA North 811](https://usanorth811.org/) — serves Northern California including San Mateo County
**Legal basis:** California Government Code §4216 (Underground Service Alert)

---

## What 811 Is

811 is the **national "Call Before You Dig" hotline** managed in Northern California by **USA North 811**. When you submit a locate request, USA North 811 notifies all member utility companies, who then send technicians to mark their underground lines at your dig site using color-coded paint or flags — at no cost to you.

---

## Is It Legally Required?

**Yes — calling 811 before digging is a legal requirement in California.**

California Government Code §4216 requires any person planning to perform excavation work to notify the regional underground service alert organization at least **two working days** before beginning. This applies to:
- Homeowners (not just contractors)
- Any digging, including fence posts, tree planting, regrading, and **footings for sheds or outbuildings**
- Any trenching for irrigation or drainage

**Violations:** Failure to call before digging can make you financially liable for any utility damage. This can easily run into tens of thousands of dollars for a gas or electrical line hit.

---

## Process Step by Step

### Step 1: Pre-mark your dig area
Before calling, mark the area where you plan to dig using **white paint, white flags, chalk, or flour**. This tells the utility locators exactly where to mark their lines.

### Step 2: Submit your locate request
- **Online (recommended):** Submit via [usanorth811.org](https://usanorth811.org/) — this is faster and avoids hold times
- **By phone:** Call **811** (toll-free, connects to USA North 811 automatically from San Mateo County)
- **Minimum notice:** Two working days before you plan to break ground. Note: weekends do not count as "working days" — if you're starting Monday, call no later than the Thursday before.

### Step 3: Wait for all utilities to respond
You **cannot start digging until all notified utility members have positively responded** to your request. Each utility has two working days to either mark their lines or issue a "no conflict" clearance.

Common utilities that will respond in San Mateo:
- **PG&E** — gas and electric
- **AT&T / Comcast / fiber providers** — telecom lines
- **City of San Mateo Public Works** — water and sewer mains
- **Private utility lines** (irrigation, etc.) — *NOT covered by 811; must be located separately*

### Step 4: Respect the markings
The colored marks indicate where utility lines run underground. The standard "tolerance zone" is **24 inches on either side** of a marked line — within this zone, you must dig carefully by hand, not with power equipment.

### Color Code for Markings

| Color | Utility |
|-------|---------|
| Red | Electric power lines |
| Yellow | Gas, oil, steam |
| Orange | Communications, alarm lines |
| Blue | Potable water |
| Purple | Reclaimed water / irrigation |
| Green | Sewer and drain lines |
| White | Proposed excavation area (pre-marked by you) |
| Pink | Temporary survey markings |

---

## Important Limitations of 811

**811 only marks lines owned by member utility companies.** It does NOT mark:
- **Private utility lines** — e.g., a gas line running from your meter to a backyard BBQ that was run by a previous owner, irrigation lines you installed yourself, etc.
- **Private sewer laterals** (the line running from your house to the city main) — these are your responsibility
- **Lines on neighboring properties**

If you're uncertain about private lines, you can hire a **private utility locating company** (typically $150–$350) to mark everything, including private lines.

Options:
1. **Shift the gazebo** — move it slightly to clear the marked tolerance zone
2. **Modify the anchoring method** — some gazebos can be surface-mounted on existing concrete using expansion anchors (no digging)
