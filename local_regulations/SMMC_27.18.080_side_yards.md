# SMMC 27.18.080 — Side Yards (R1 District)

**Source:** [https://law.cityofsanmateo.org/us/ca/cities/san-mateo/code/27.18.080](https://law.cityofsanmateo.org/us/ca/cities/san-mateo/code/27.18.080)
**Chapter:** 27.18 — One-Family Dwelling (R1) District Regulations

---

## Full Text (as extracted)

### Interior Side Yards

> "Not less than seven (7) feet in an R1-A district and not less than five (5) feet in an R1-B or R1-C district."

### Street Side Yards (Corner Parcels Only)

**Ground floors:**
> "Fifteen percent (15%) of the lot width as measured at building location, not less than 7.5 feet and not to exceed 25 feet in the R1-A district and 15 feet in the R1-B and C districts."

**Upper stories:**
> Minimum of ten (10) feet.

**Garages:**
> "Minimum of twenty (20) feet."

### Paving Restrictions in Street Side Yards

- Single-car garages: 17-foot maximum width or 40% of street frontage (whichever is less)
- Two-car or larger garages: 20-foot maximum width
- Pedestrian walkways: five (5) feet or less in width

Exception: Single-car garages setback 35+ feet from street require no additional paving beyond the driveway.

### Non-Conforming Single-Story Horizontal Extensions

Single-story horizontal additions may maintain an existing setback line (i.e., can be non-conforming) if:
- The existing yard is at least 3 feet from the property line
- Maximum height: 12 feet
- Maximum extension length: 20 feet

### Non-Residential Uses in R1 Zones

> "Minimum side yards, both interior and street, of fifteen (15) feet or one-half the building height, whichever is greater."

## Summary & Relevance to Project

**What this means for the gazebo:**

The standard interior side yard setback for the property at 318 24th Ave (R1-C district) is **5 feet** from the property line.

A 1–2 foot placement from the side fence would violate this requirement **unless** the gazebo qualifies for the rear-one-third exemption in SMMC 27.18.100 (see that file for details).

**Important distinction:** Setbacks are measured from the **property line**, not from the fence. Fences are often not precisely on the property line. The actual gap between the gazebo and the property line could differ from the gap between the gazebo and the fence.
