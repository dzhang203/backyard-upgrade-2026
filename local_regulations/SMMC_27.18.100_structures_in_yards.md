# SMMC 27.18.100 — Structures and Building Projections in Required Yards

**Source:** [https://law.cityofsanmateo.org/us/ca/cities/san-mateo/code/27.18.100](https://law.cityofsanmateo.org/us/ca/cities/san-mateo/code/27.18.100)
**Chapter:** 27.18 — One-Family Dwelling (R1) District Regulations

---

## Key Provision: Rear One-Third Exemption

Per the San Mateo Law Library and confirmed via official city search results, Section 27.18.100 contains the following rule (paraphrased from official source language):

> A detached accessory building located within the **rear one-third of a parcel** is exempt from the requirements for interior side and rear yards, provided that the structure:
>
> 1. Is separated from the principal building by a distance of **not less than four (4) feet** that is open to the sky
> 2. Shall not exceed a height of **nine (9) feet to the plate line** and **sixteen (16) feet to the roof peak**
> 3. Shall not extend above or beyond a daylight plane having a height of **nine (9) feet at each side and rear property line** and extending into the parcel at an angle of **forty-five (45) degrees**

Additionally:

> Accessory buildings shall not occupy more than **fifty (50) percent of a required rear yard.**

## Annotated Conditions

### Condition 1: Must be in the Rear One-Third of the Lot

"Rear one-third" is measured from the **rear property line**, not from the fence or the edge of the backyard.

**For 318 24th Ave:**
- Lot: ~0.1527 acres ≈ 6,650 sq ft
- Assumed lot width: ~50 ft (typical for this area)
- Implied lot depth: ~133 ft
- Rear one-third: the rear **~44 feet** of the lot measured from the rear property line

**Action:** Physically measure from the rear fence inward. If the **entire gazebo footprint** (not just the closest corner) falls within 44 feet of the rear fence, the exemption applies.

### Condition 2: 4-Foot Clearance from House

The gazebo must be separated from the principal dwelling (the main house) by at least 4 feet of open-to-sky space. This is a **fire separation requirement**, not just a setback.

From the design plans, this appears to be met easily — the gazebo is located near the far end of the patio, well away from the house.

### Condition 3: Plate Line ≤ 9 Feet

The **plate line** (also called eave height) is the point where the roof framing meets the top of the exterior wall — essentially the bottom of the roof. On the proposed gazebo (a double-tier hardtop), this corresponds to the top of the main support columns.

**For the specific gazebo product (Gazebo.png):**
- Typical 10×16 hardtop aluminum gazebos from major manufacturers (e.g., Sunjoy, AECOJOY, Palram) have eave heights ranging from **8'6" to 9'6"**
- The double-tier design adds a second ventilation layer on top, but this doesn't change the plate line measurement
- **You must get the manufacturer's spec sheet** and confirm the eave/plate height is ≤ 9'0" for this exemption to apply

### Condition 4: Roof Peak ≤ 16 Feet

The highest point of the roof must not exceed 16 feet. Most pre-manufactured 10×16 hardtop gazebos peak at 10–12 feet, well within this limit.

### Condition 5: 45-Degree Daylight Plane

The structure must stay within an imaginary slope that starts at 9 feet height at the property line and rises at 45 degrees inward. In practice, for a gazebo this means: if the peak of the roof is set back X feet from the property line, the roof peak must be no taller than (9 + X) feet. This condition is almost certainly met for a 10–12 ft peak gazebo set at or near the property line.

### Condition 6: ≤ 50% of Required Rear Yard Coverage

The required rear yard in R1-C is 15 feet deep. So the maximum area that accessory buildings can cover within the rear 15 feet = 50% of (lot width × 15 ft). For a ~50 ft wide lot: max coverage = 375 sq ft. The gazebo at 160 sq ft is well within this limit (especially since it's not in the required rear yard — it's likely in the area beyond the required 15 ft setback).

## Decision Tree

```
Is the gazebo located in the rear 1/3 of the lot?
├── NO → 5 ft side yard setback applies. 1–2 ft placement is a code violation.
└── YES → Is the eave/plate height ≤ 9 feet?
    ├── NO → 5 ft side yard setback applies. 1–2 ft placement is a code violation.
    └── YES → Is there ≥ 4 ft clearance from the house?
        ├── NO → Does not qualify for exemption.
        └── YES → EXEMPTION APPLIES → Zero setback required. Gazebo may go to property line.
```

## Other Projections Allowed in Required Yards (General)

The section also covers these standard encroachments into required yards (applicable regardless of the rear-1/3 exemption):
- Eaves, cornices, awnings: may project up to 2.5 ft into a required yard
- Chimneys, bay windows, fireplaces: typically minor projections allowed per code

## Summary

This is the most important code section for the project. The rear-one-third exemption is the key to whether the gazebo can legally be placed within 1–2 feet of the side fence. The two conditions that need verification before proceeding:

1. **Confirm the gazebo footprint falls entirely within the rear 1/3 of the lot** (measure from rear property line)
2. **Confirm the specific gazebo product has a plate/eave height ≤ 9 feet** (get spec sheet)
