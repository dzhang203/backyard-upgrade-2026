## Full Extracted Text (from city web page)

### Placement Restrictions

Accessory structures are subject to the following placement rules:

- **Front yard:** Not permitted in required front yard setbacks
- **Front two-thirds of side yards:** Not permitted
- **Corner lots:** Not permitted in required street-side setback areas
- **Rear one-third of the lot:** Permitted in side or rear yards, potentially right up to the property line (subject to height conditions in SMMC 27.18.100)
- **Setback areas within the rear third:** Structures here face "stringent height limitations" — see SMMC 27.18.100

### Permit Requirements

> "A building permit is required for any storage shed or accessory building **over 120 square feet** in area."

Structures **under 120 sq ft**:
- No building permit required
- But setback rules still apply for the front two-thirds of the lot

### Easement Restrictions

> "Accessory buildings shall not be constructed within any recorded easements **without the written approval of the agency who has jurisdiction** (Department of Public Works, PG&E, etc.)."

The city notes that property owners can identify easement locations by:
- Viewing underground utility maps at the public counter at City Hall
- Reviewing parcel maps at the Public Works counter

### Application Requirements

When applying for a building permit for an accessory building, applicants must provide:
1. A site plan showing the proposed construction location
2. Lot size and the location of all existing buildings
3. Distance measurements from all structures to property lines
4. Four sets of building plans for review

## Summary & Relevance to Project

This page is the city's primary practical guide for accessory buildings. Key takeaways:

1. **The 120 sq ft threshold is firm** — a 160 sq ft gazebo needs a permit, no exceptions on the face of this page.

2. **Rear-third placement can go to zero setback** — the city itself acknowledges this, pointing to SMMC 27.18.100 for the height conditions.

3. **Easement check is mandatory** — the city explicitly requires written approval from the relevant utility agency (PG&E, Public Works, etc.) before constructing within any recorded easement. This is not discretionary. If there's an easement along the side fence, the gazebo cannot be installed there without written utility company consent.

4. **Site plan required for permit** — the building permit application must show exact distances from all structures to property lines. The city uses this to verify setback compliance (or the rear-third exemption).
