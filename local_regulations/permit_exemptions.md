# City of San Mateo — Building Permit Exemptions

**Source:** [https://www.cityofsanmateo.org/FAQ.aspx?QID=86](https://www.cityofsanmateo.org/FAQ.aspx?QID=86)
**Related:** [https://www.cityofsanmateo.org/479/Building-Permits](https://www.cityofsanmateo.org/479/Building-Permits)

## Full Extracted List of Permit-Exempt Items

### Structures

- **One-story detached accessory buildings** where the floor area does **not exceed 120 square feet** — no permit required
- **Platforms, decks, and walks** not exceeding 200 square feet, not more than 30 inches above grade, not attached to a dwelling, and not serving as an exit from a door — no permit required *(Note: this exemption does not override Planning Department regulations — impervious surface limits still apply)*

### Fences

- **Rear fences** up to **7 feet** in height — no permit required
- **Front fences** up to **3 feet** in height — no permit required

### Minor Structures / Work

- Painting, papering, tiling, carpeting, and similar finish work
- Temporary theatrical sets and scenery
- Window awnings supported by an exterior wall, not projecting more than 54 inches

### Swimming Pools

- Above-ground swimming pools under 5,000 gallons — no permit required

---

## What Is NOT Listed as Exempt (Relevant to This Project)

- **Gazebos and patio covers** — not mentioned as exempt. Since they don't appear in the exemption list and are over 120 sq ft, a permit is required.
- **Attached patio covers** — explicitly require a permit (per city building department guidance).
- **Pre-manufactured structures** — the fact that it's a kit/pre-fab structure does not grant an exemption.

---

## California Residential Code Context

San Mateo has adopted the **2022 California Residential Code (CRC)**, which includes **Appendix AH — Patio Covers**. Under this appendix:
- Patio covers are defined as one-story structures not exceeding 12 feet in height, open on at least two sides
- Even patio covers must comply with setback and structural requirements
- Permit is required if over 120 sq ft (per San Mateo's local threshold)

A hardtop aluminum gazebo (like the one pictured in Gazebo.png) with curtains would likely be classified either as a **patio cover** (if primarily open on 2+ sides) or as an **accessory structure**. Either way, at 160 sq ft it requires a permit.

## Summary & Relevance to Project

The key numbers:
- **Gazebo:** 10×16 = 160 sq ft → **Permit required** (exceeds 120 sq ft threshold)
- **Paver expansion:** Ground-level, under 30" → **Permit likely NOT required** (fits deck/walk exemption), but check impervious surface limits
- **Fence repair/replacement:** Standard height fences → **No permit required**

The permit exemption for structures under 120 sq ft is the only realistic path to avoiding a building permit. The only way the gazebo could fit under this exemption would be to reduce to a smaller footprint (e.g., 10×12 = 120 sq ft — right at the edge, and still likely requires permit since the FAQ says "does not exceed"). A 10×10 = 100 sq ft structure would clearly be exempt.
