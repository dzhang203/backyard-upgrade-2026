## 1. Gazebo Permit

**A building permit is required.** San Mateo requires a permit for any accessory structure over **120 sq ft**. The 10×16 gazebo is 160 sq ft.

Key points:

- San Mateo has adopted the **2022 California Residential Code**, including **Appendix AH (Patio Covers)**.
- Pre-manufactured/kit gazebos are not exempt — the permit covers footings and anchoring.
- Application requires: site plan, lot dimensions, distances from structures to property lines, and product specifications.

Apply via the [City of San Mateo Online Permit Center](https://www.cityofsanmateo.org/4294/Online-Permit-Center). Contacts: Building (650) 522-7172, Planning (650) 522-7202.

## 2. Side Yard Setback

### Standard R1-C Rule

Per **SMMC 27.18.080**, the standard interior side setback in R1-C is **5 feet**.

### The "Rear One-Third" Exemption (SMMC 27.18.100)

A detached accessory building in the **rear one-third of the parcel** is **exempt from side and rear setback requirements** (can go to property line), provided:

1. **≥4 ft open-to-sky clearance** from the principal building.
2. **Plate (eave) height ≤9 ft.**
3. **Peak height ≤16 ft.**
4. **Daylight plane:** does not extend above a 45° plane rising from 9 ft at each side/rear property line.
5. **Rear yard coverage:** accessory buildings occupy ≤50% of the required rear yard.

### Does the proposed gazebo qualify?

| Criterion | Status |
|---|---|
| In rear 1/3 of parcel | Likely yes — needs on-site measurement |
| Plate (eave) height | Purple Leaf eave is **82" ≈ 6'10"** — well under 9 ft ✓ |
| Peak height | Purple Leaf peak is **114" ≈ 9'6"** — well under 16 ft ✓ |
| 4 ft clearance from house | Design target — verify placement |
| Daylight plane | Easily met at 9'6" peak and 3 ft fence offset |
| ≤50% rear yard coverage | 160 sq ft well under typical rear-yard area |

If the rear-1/3 position is confirmed, **0 ft side setback is legal**. Current plan is 3 ft from fence — voluntarily above the legal minimum for maintenance and moisture reasons (see `fence_proximity_analysis.md`).

## 3. Fence-Adjacent Planter (Shaded, NW of Gazebo)

The planter just northwest of the gazebo will sit in gazebo shadow most of the day:

- Afternoon shade from the gazebo roof (west sun blocked).
- Little direct morning sun (lot and fence orientation).
- Bay Area cool/foggy summers further reduce incident light.

Expect **2–4 hours of indirect / partial sun** in summer, very little in winter.

### Realistic options

**Leafy edibles** (tolerate partial shade; prefer cool summers): lettuce, spinach, arugula, chard, kale. Herbs: mint, parsley, cilantro, chives. Skip fruiting crops (tomatoes, peppers, squash) — they need 6+ hours.

**Ornamentals:** sword fern, maidenhair fern, coral bells (Heuchera), Japanese forest grass (Hakonechloa), bleeding heart (Dicentra), hydrangea.

Frame this as a **"cool-season greens + herbs + shade ornamentals"** bed, not a general vegetable garden.

## 4. Paver Expansion (Outside Gazebo)

- Ground-level pavers (under 30" above grade) generally **do not require a building permit** in San Mateo.
- Must comply with Planning Dept. **impervious surface limits** — typically 50% in R1 zones.
- Dry-laid paver systems with sand joints are treated more favorably than poured concrete under stormwater rules; confirm during permit review.
